Trust

is a foundation of agility

Trust is elevated by knowing what information assets you have, where they originated from, and who can verify or explain the data. Agility also depends on the ability to easily consume, validate, and transform data for use in analytics and business intelligence.

Our name is derived from meta, about the structure and definition of a thing, and morphic, of changing from one form into another. Metamorphic is a platform for:

Multistructured Content

Profile, search and transform data from sources including databases, Excel and files in multiple formats

Metadata

Discover and manage metadata (data about data) including structure, definitions and quality attributes using machine learning

Data Quality

Validate data quality using behaviour-driven specifications and automated tests to create a data quality firewall

At Scale

Operate at scale to support big data and streaming events with connectors for Hadoop and NoSQL such as MongoDB

About

Our goal is to support analytic-driven organizations by providing a scalable solution for managing metadata and enabling the agile transformation of data in multi-vendor environments.

Mark Moloney
Founder

Bio

The first half of my career was shaped from my experience as a management consultant working with large organizations in future visioning, information strategy, and business analysis during which time I was fortunate to live/work in many countries and across a range of industries.

I gravitated towards developing the enabling technologies to see the vision through to implementation. Examples of the systems I have designed and had overall accountability for delivery include the settlement and payments systems for a Reserve Bank, an all-of-government authentication system, a mobile banking and payments framework ranked as the leading platform in the US, and enterprise data warehouses in banking, telecommunications and retail.

Five years ago, my interest began to focus on using the growing volume of information that was being collected - often treated as a byproduct of transactional systems. I co-founded a company, which provided findability solutions (search based applications) and developed a semantic search product to improve the level of information recall from enterprise document stores.

I believe that there are tremendous opportunities from instant recall of knowledge and machine augmented analysis and learning. These opportunities depend on the ability to consume and analyze vast quantities of data, which in turn, depends on the ability to discover and manage data about data (metadata).
